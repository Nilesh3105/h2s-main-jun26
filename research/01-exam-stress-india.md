## Key Takeaways

- **Student suicides have nearly doubled in a decade.** NCRB recorded **13,044 student suicides in 2022**, up from ~6,654 a decade earlier — and student suicides rose ~4%/yr vs ~2%/yr for total suicides, i.e. *faster than the youth population grew*. ([IC3 Institute report, 2024, using NCRB 2022 data](https://ic3institute.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Student-suicides-report-Digital.pdf))
- **Exam-specific deaths are smaller but real.** Of India's **170,924 total suicides in 2022**, **7.6% (~13,000) were students** and **2,248 deaths were attributed to "failure in examination"**; exam-failure cases are ~1% of all suicides (down from ~2% the prior decade). ([Lancet Reg. Health SE Asia, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/); [NCRB 2022 analysis, Indian J Psychol Med, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11558707/))
- **Kota is the canonical coaching-hub flashpoint.** Kota recorded **~26–29 student suicides in 2023** (a multi-year high) and **17 in 2024** (a reported ~50% drop after interventions), against **200,000+ students/year** in **300+ coaching centres**. ([The Diplomat, 2024](https://thediplomat.com/2024/02/student-suicides-in-kotas-coaching-factories-point-to-indias-broken-education-system/); [Lancet, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))
- **Anxiety/depression prevalence among aspirants is very high in the (mostly small) study literature** — single studies of NEET aspirants report **anxiety ~40–100%** and **depression ~44–59%**; treat exact figures as study-specific, not national. ([IJCMPH, NEET Tamil Nadu](https://www.ijcmph.com/index.php/ijcmph/article/view/9481); [JCDR, NEET Chennai](https://www.jcdr.net/articles/PDF/17288/60039_CE(AD)_F%5BSK%5D_PF1_(JY_SS)_PFA(SS)_PN(SHU).pdf))
- **Results season produces a measurable spike.** A Chennai suicide-prevention NGO (SNEHA) documented a **fourfold increase in calls/visits from suicidal students in May**, when public-exam results are announced. ([Lancet Reg. Health SE Asia, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))
- **The concrete top triggers are psychosocial, not just academic.** A Kota study of 240 students flagged **performance-contingent self-worth (rank = identity), absence of a confidant, and sleeping <6 hours** as the strongest predictors of severe distress — stronger than academic performance itself. ([summarized from drakashpariharkota.in / aligned with peer-reviewed Kota work](https://drakashpariharkota.in/student-mental-health-kota-coaching/)) **[single-study / media-summarized — verify]**
- **Support is structurally inadequate.** India has **~0.75 psychiatrists per 100,000 people** (WHO norm: 3), and the National Mental Health Survey found a **treatment gap of ~70–92%** for mental disorders. ([NMHS 2015–16, Int J Soc Psychiatry, 2020](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32126902/)) **[NMHS data is 2015–16 — dated but still the national benchmark]**
- **Stigma is the dominant barrier to help-seeking, especially for the young.** A systematic review of stigma among young people in India found stigma is a leading reason youth don't seek care; **<10% of youth access mental-health services** per recent multi-university work. ([BMC Psychiatry systematic review, 2020](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12888-020-02937-x))

## 1. Prevalence of Anxiety, Depression, Stress & Burnout Among Aspirants

**Important framing:** there is **no single national prevalence figure specific to "exam aspirants."** What exists is (a) the National Mental Health Survey for the general population, and (b) many *small, local, cross-sectional* studies of specific aspirant cohorts. The aspirant-specific numbers are high but should be cited as **study-specific**, not generalizable.

### General-population baseline (national, authoritative but dated)
- **NMHS 2015–16:** lifetime prevalence of *any* mental morbidity **13.67%**, current prevalence **10.56%**; ~150 million Indians affected. Most common categories: substance-use disorders, mood disorders (5.61%), neurotic & stress-related disorders (3.70%). ([NMHS, Int J Soc Psychiatry, 2020](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32126902/) / [methods paper, 2018](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6201882/)) **[data 2015–16 — dated]**

### Aspirant-specific studies (small cohorts — cite as single studies)
- **NEET, Chennai (n≈250):** depressive symptoms in **59.2%**; anxiety symptoms reported in the cohort at very high rates. ([JCDR](https://www.jcdr.net/articles/PDF/17288/60039_CE(AD)_F%5BSK%5D_PF1_(JY_SS)_PFA(SS)_PN(SHU).pdf); [DOAJ listing](https://doaj.org/article/6dd794e88a904ab98cb2e6fc8ca2c3b3))
- **NEET, rural vs urban Tamil Nadu (n≈150):** anxiety **40% (urban) / 60% (rural)**; depression **45.3% (urban) / 56% (rural)** — i.e. rural aspirants worse off. ([IJCMPH](https://www.ijcmph.com/index.php/ijcmph/article/view/9481))
- **NEET, Aligarh (n≈220):** *extremely severe* anxiety **37.7%**, severe depression **20%**, high stress **10.9%**; anxiety higher among females. **[single study — verify n and instrument]**
- **UPSC CSE aspirants (survey-based):** **53.3%** rated their mental health poor/somewhat poor; **41.7%** reported emotional problems interfering with work/daily life; **36%** rated physical health poor. ([IJRASET survey study](https://www.ijraset.com/research-paper/mental-health-status-of-upsc-cse-aspirants); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/371171956_Mental_Health_Status_of_UPSC_CSE_Aspirants_A_Survey-Based_Study)) **[non-indexed/low-tier journal — treat as indicative]**
- **Broad student samples (not aspirant-specific):** a 2025 multi-university survey (n≈8,542, 30 universities, 9 states) found moderate-to-high **anxiety 69.9%, depression 59.9%, distress 70.3%**, with **<10% accessing services**. ([summarized via Mentis/MHFA India reporting](https://www.mhfaindia.com/blog/college-university/student-mental-health-crisis-indian-campuses-support)) **[locate the primary peer-reviewed study before quoting the 69.9% figure]**

> **Caveat on the "90% anxiety / 79% depression" and "65% stress / 42% depression" figures** that circulate in coaching/edtech blogs: I could **not** trace these to a primary peer-reviewed source. Treat as **[unverified]** marketing-grade claims; do not quote.

**CAT / GATE / CUET specifically:** dedicated peer-reviewed prevalence studies are sparse. Most "CAT/GATE stress" content is journalistic or blog-grade. **[gap — flag as unverified; do not assign a number]**

## 2. The Kota Coaching Ecosystem & Suicide Statistics

### The ecosystem
- **200,000+ students per year** migrate to Kota, Rajasthan, enrolling across **300+ coaching centres**, primarily for JEE (engineering) and NEET (medical). ([Lancet Reg. Health SE Asia, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))

### Reported suicide counts (note: Kota figures come from district/police tallies and media, not a single clean official series)
- **2023:** widely reported as **~26–29** student suicides — the highest in years (some sources say "highest since 2015"). ([Lancet, 2024: "29 suicides in Kota in 2023"](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/); [The Diplomat, 2024](https://thediplomat.com/2024/02/student-suicides-in-kotas-coaching-factories-point-to-indias-broken-education-system/))
- **2024:** **17** coaching-student suicides — district administration claimed a **~50% decline** vs 2023. ([reported via journals.sagepub Kota study, 2025](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/09710973251356797))
- **2025:** at least one case reported in January 2025; full-year official tally not yet confirmed at time of writing. ([India TV, Jan 2025](https://www.indiatvnews.com/rajasthan/kota-coaching-student-from-haryana-commits-suicide-father-demands-probe-2025-01-08-970271)) **[2025 full-year number unverified]**

> Note the **discrepancy between sources (26 vs 29 for 2023)** — exact Kota counts vary by who is counting (police vs administration vs media) and by definition (coaching students only vs all student deaths). Quote a **range** and cite the source.

### Government/institutional response (context for a wellness product)
- **Jan 2024:** Union Ministry of Education issued national **guidelines regulating private coaching centres**. ([Lancet, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))
- Rajasthan measures: mandatory **"suicide-proofing" spring-loaded ceiling fans** in hostels, **suspension of routine/competitive tests for ~2 months**, mandatory trained counsellors, teacher mental-health training. ([The Diplomat, 2024](https://thediplomat.com/2024/02/student-suicides-in-kotas-coaching-factories-point-to-indias-broken-education-system/))

## 3. Specific Stress Triggers (for trigger-tagging design)

This is the most product-relevant section. Below are concrete, grounded triggers, each mapped to evidence. Where a trigger is supported only by media/clinical commentary rather than a controlled study, it is flagged.

| Trigger | Evidence basis | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Mock-test / rank results** ("performance-contingent self-worth" — defining identity by rank) | Cited as a *top* predictor of severe distress in Kota cohort discussion | [Kota mental-health writeup](https://drakashpariharkota.in/student-mental-health-kota-coaching/) **[verify against primary study]** |
| **Sleep deprivation (<6 hrs/night)** | Named among strongest distress predictors; general sleep-stress literature supports | [Kota writeup](https://drakashpariharkota.in/student-mental-health-kota-coaching/); [sleep/academic study, 2021](https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2021.09.19.21263802.full.pdf) |
| **Social isolation / "no confidant"** | Absence of a trusted person to talk to flagged as a leading predictor | [Kota writeup](https://drakashpariharkota.in/student-mental-health-kota-coaching/) **[verify]** |
| **Parental pressure / over-ambitious expectations** | NCRB analysis lists "over-anxious and over-ambitious parents, comparisons with peers, lack of family support" among contributing factors | [Lancet/NCRB analysis, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/) |
| **Peer comparison / competitive ranking culture** | "Hyper-competitive environments," peer comparison repeatedly cited | [NCRB 2022 contributing factors](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11558707/); [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2024/02/student-suicides-in-kotas-coaching-factories-point-to-indias-broken-education-system/) |
| **Fear of failure / repeated attempts (droppers)** | Rural NEET study: anxiety/depression *higher among repeat attempters* | [IJCMPH](https://www.ijcmph.com/index.php/ijcmph/article/view/9481) |
| **Financial pressure on family** | Aspirants from humble backgrounds; financial crisis named as a reported suicide reason | [UPSC study](https://www.ijraset.com/research-paper/mental-health-status-of-upsc-cse-aspirants); [Kota suicide-reason analysis](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/09710973251356797) |
| **Institutional/social harassment** (bullying, ragging, caste discrimination, toxic culture) | Listed as institutional contributing factors in student-suicide newspaper analysis | [Student Suicide newspaper analysis, PubMed, 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39380363/) |

**Design implication:** the evidence points to **psychosocial triggers (rank-as-identity, no confidant, sleep loss)** being at least as predictive as raw academic performance — a trigger-tagging taxonomy should include emotional/social/sleep tags, not just "exam date" or "marks."

## 4. Result-Season / Post-Result Mental-Health Spikes

- **Fourfold (4x) increase** in calls/visits from suicidal students in **May**, the public-exam results month, per Chennai NGO **SNEHA**. Students who *failed in one or two subjects* were over-represented among deaths. ([Lancet Reg. Health SE Asia, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))
- **Targeted result-window interventions exist and work:** Karnataka, with **NIMHANS**, ran a helpline giving psychosocial support to Class 10 students *before, during, and after* result announcement — a model timed precisely to the spike. ([PMC: matriculation-result helpline model, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12349869/))
- **Policy that flattens the spike:** Tamil Nadu's **supplementary-exam** scheme (since 2003) is credited with **~70% reduction in exam-failure suicides statewide and ~82% in Chennai**; in 2023, **127,622** sat the supplementary exam and **40% passed**. ([Lancet, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/)) **[origin policy is 2003 — dated, but the 2023 throughput data is current]**
- **2026 context (CBSE results controversy):** the May 2026 CBSE results — affecting **1.7M+ students** — drew criticism over a glitchy digitised evaluation, heightening result-season anxiety. ([Al Jazeera, Jun 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/4/how-indias-cbse-exam-scandal-set-off-student-outrage-against-pm-modi)) **[breaking news — corroborate before quoting]**

**Design implication:** mental-health risk is **seasonally clustered** around result dates (notably May for boards). A wellness tracker should weight risk scoring / proactive outreach to result windows.

## 5. Why Existing Support Is Inadequate

- **Severe workforce shortage:** **~0.75 psychiatrists per 100,000** vs the WHO norm of **3** — roughly 1 psychiatrist per ~150,000 people. ([widely reported; aligns with NMHS workforce findings](https://soondra.org/2025/04/04/mental-healthcare-in-india-barriers-legal-rights-and-solutions/); see NMHS for primary). **[secondary source — pin to NMHS/govt primary before quoting]**
- **Treatment gap ~70–92%:** NMHS 2015–16 found a treatment gap of **70.4–86.3%** depending on disorder (overall ~83–85%). ([NMHS, 2020](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32126902/)) **[data 2015–16 — dated]**
- **Stigma is the dominant help-seeking barrier**, particularly among youth — fear of marriage/employment consequences and family shame. ([BMC Psychiatry systematic review of stigma in young people, 2020](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12888-020-02937-x))
- **Low service uptake:** recent multi-university work reports **<10% of youth access mental-health services**, and only ~41% believe in seeking support. ([MHFA India summary](https://www.mhfaindia.com/blog/college-university/student-mental-health-crisis-indian-campuses-support)) **[locate primary study]**
- **National responses are nascent / under-utilised:** **Tele-MANAS** (launched Oct 2022) had handled **~29.8 lakh (~3 million) calls by Dec 2025**; ~80% of calls were routine concerns (anxiety, stress, sleep) and ~70% of callers were aged 18–45. The **National Suicide Prevention Strategy (NSPS)** launched 2022; **NEP 2020** addresses well-being but implementation is limited. ([The Hans India / govt, Dec 2025](https://www.thehansindia.com/news/national/tele-manas-mental-health-helpline-handles-over-297-lakh-calls-since-2022-1027657); [Lancet, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11268324/))

**Design implication:** the gap a product fills is **access + privacy/anti-stigma + early, low-friction, self-serve detection** — formal psychiatric care is scarce, stigmatised, and reactive.

## Caveats & Data-Quality Notes

- **NCRB data** is the authoritative *suicide* source but is widely held to **undercount** (police-reported; stigma; misclassification). Latest full year cited here is **2022**; 2023/2024 NCRB student figures were not confirmed to NCRB primary at time of writing.
- **NMHS** (the national prevalence benchmark) is from **2015–16** — dated. A second NMHS round has been discussed; verify whether newer national data exists before publishing.
- **Aspirant-specific prevalence** rests on **small, single-site, cross-sectional** studies with varying instruments (GAD-7, PHQ-9, DASS-21) — high heterogeneity. Report ranges, not point estimates.
- **Kota counts** vary by source/definition; quote a **range with citation**.
- Several circulating figures ("90% anxiety," "65% stress / 42% depression," exact "0.75 psychiatrists") appear in **secondary/blog** sources; I flagged these to pin to primaries before any external use.
